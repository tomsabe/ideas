# Halozyme Therapeutics: A Royalty Machine Trading at a Discount to Its Own Growth

*Shares at ~$64 imply roughly 6x 2026 adjusted EBITDA — a striking disconnect for a business with rapidly expanding margins, $1B+ in royalty revenue on deck, and a free option on the Merck/Keytruda dispute.*

---

## A Pull-Back Creates an Entry Point

Halozyme's 52-week range spans $47.50 to $82.22. After hitting that high early in 2026, the stock has retreated roughly 23%, with shares now trading around $63–64. The sell-off is not driven by any deterioration in fundamentals. In fact, the business just delivered its best year ever, and management raised guidance sharply. The disconnect between operational momentum and share price is the thesis in one sentence: *the market is re-rating the stock based on near-term noise while the underlying royalty engine compounds at an accelerating rate.*

The case for HALO rests on three legs: (1) an ENHANZE royalty stream still early in its penetration curve despite already approaching $1 billion annually; (2) two platform acquisitions that extend the revenue runway deep into the 2040s; and (3) a high-upside asymmetric option embedded in the Merck/Keytruda intellectual property dispute that is entirely absent from consensus models.

## A Year of Records

In 2025, Halozyme posted revenue of $1.40 billion, up 38% from the prior year's $1.015 billion. More important than the headline was the composition. Full-year royalty revenue reached a record $867.8 million — and royalties tell the cleaner story.

| Royalty Stream | FY2025 ($M) | FY2024 ($M) | YoY Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| DARZALEX SC | $483 | $375 | +29% |
| VYVGART Hytrulo | $157 | $29 | +444% |
| Phesgo | $106 | $70 | +51% |
| All Other | $122 | $97 | +26% |
| **Total Royalties** | **$868** | **$571** | **+52%** |

Two things stand out. VYVGART Hytrulo — argenx's FcRn inhibitor for autoimmune diseases — grew 444% in a single year and is genuinely early in its commercial life. Meanwhile DARZALEX, already Halozyme's largest royalty contributor, is still growing at 29% annually as subcutaneous adoption deepens and new indications gain approval.

The broader ENHANZE platform continued to add products. In April 2025, the European Commission approved subcutaneous RYBREVANT, marking the tenth partnered ENHANZE product to reach commercialization. The FDA followed with U.S. approval of RYBREVANT FASPRO in December 2025, further expanding the royalty base.

## 2026 Guidance: The Numbers That Matter

Management raised full-year 2026 guidance to:

- **Total revenue:** $1.71–$1.81 billion (23–30% growth)
- **Royalty revenue:** $1.13–$1.17 billion (30–35% growth)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $1.125–$1.205 billion
- **Non-GAAP diluted EPS:** $7.75–$8.25

Royalty revenue surpassing $1 billion was originally expected in 2027. That milestone arriving a full year early underscores the compounding power of the platform.

At the current share price of ~$64 and an enterprise value of roughly $7.4 billion, the stock trades at approximately 6.3x the midpoint of 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance and about 8x forward non-GAAP earnings. For a royalty-dominant business projecting EBITDA margins of 71–73% by 2027–2028 and free cash flow conversion exceeding 70% of EBITDA, those multiples are remarkably compressed — particularly when compared to specialty pharma royalty peers that often trade at 12–18x EBITDA on far less visible revenue streams.

The multi-year trajectory is even more compelling:

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue ($M) | $1,015 | $1,397 | $1,710–$1,810 | $1,920–$2,045 | $2,045–$2,170 |

*\*FY2025 adjusted EBITDA affected by one-time acquisition-related charges.*

Management's guidance implies approximately 19–21% revenue CAGR and 26–27% non-GAAP EPS CAGR from 2024 to 2028 — a growth profile that simply does not reconcile with a sub-10x EBITDA multiple.

## The ENHANZE Engine Still Has Fuel

A common bear argument is that ENHANZE's patent protection begins rolling off around 2027 in the U.S. and 2029 in Europe, creating a royalty rate step-down that crimps the growth story. This concern is real but materially overstated.

**New products are still in early adoption.** OCREVUS Zunovo, Tecentriq Hybreza, Opdivo Qvantig, and RYBREVANT Faspro all received approvals within the last 12–18 months. These launches are early in their adoption curves, especially internationally, and represent diversified royalty streams that should drive sequential revenue growth.

**DARZALEX has contractual protection beyond the standard patent term.** Janssen's royalty term is structured as 12 years post first commercial sale (2020), meaning DARZALEX royalties run to 2032 regardless of ENHANZE patent expiry — a fact that is frequently mis-modeled by sell-side analysts.

**New collaborations expand the base.** In late 2025, Halozyme signed three new ENHANZE collaboration agreements — with Takeda (for vedolizumab/ENTYVIO), Merus, and Skye Bioscience. The company expects 1–3 additional ENHANZE deals in 2026, plus 1–2 new Hypercon partnerships.

## Platform Expansion: The 2040s Story Priced at Zero

When Halozyme paid $750 million upfront for Elektrofi (Hypercon technology) in November 2025 and up to $400 million for Surf Bio in December 2025, the market focused on near-term dilution. The longer-term strategic logic is substantially more valuable than the headlines suggested.

**Hypercon** is a microparticle technology that concentrates biologics to 400–500 mg/mL — four to five times the concentration of standard formulations. This enables the same dose in a dramatically smaller injection volume, opening the door to at-home administration via auto-injector for drugs currently requiring hospital infusions. Halozyme already has three Hypercon partners: Janssen, Lilly, and argenx. Two partners are expected to begin Phase 1 clinical trials for Hypercon-formulated products by end of 2026.

Both Hypercon and Surf Bio carry patent portfolios extending into the mid-2040s — well beyond any ENHANZE patent concern. Management has outlined a path to approximately $1 billion in Hypercon royalty revenues by the mid-2030s, driven by first approvals projected in 2030–2031 and 3–5 additional product launches thereafter.

Current consensus models treat these platforms as speculative long-duration options. That characterization is partially correct — the revenues are indeed 2030+. But framing them as worthless in a present-value sense ignores validated feasibility with blockbuster drugs already in development, a regulatory path that management has described as clinic-ready, and an IP fortress extending nearly two decades. Even a conservative probability-adjusted DCF contribution from these platforms would add several dollars per share to fair value.

## The Merck Wild Card

Perhaps the most underappreciated element of the HALO thesis is the ongoing intellectual property dispute with Merck over Keytruda SC.

Halozyme filed a patent infringement lawsuit against Merck in U.S. District Court in New Jersey, alleging that the subcutaneous formulation of Keytruda infringes multiple MDASE patents. Keytruda is the world's best-selling oncology drug, with 2025 sales of $31.7 billion. Merck developed its subcutaneous version — marketed as Keytruda Qlex in the U.S. — using Alteogen's ALT-B4 hyaluronidase. Halozyme contends that the modifications in ALT-B4 fall within the scope of its MDASE patents, which cover thousands of modified human hyaluronidase variants researched by Halozyme scientists.

Critically, the MDASE patents are entirely separate from the ENHANZE licensing program and are not included in ENHANZE licenses — meaning the outcome of this dispute has no adverse impact on existing royalty streams.

In December 2025, the Munich Regional Court's 7th Civil Division granted Halozyme a preliminary injunction, ordering Merck to halt Keytruda SC launch activities in Germany. The court found imminent infringement regarding European Patent No. 2 797 622. That is a meaningful indicator of patent strength in a jurisdiction not known for issuing preliminary injunctions lightly.

Halozyme's CEO has stated publicly that she hoped the companies could strike a licensing deal at a "reasonable" royalty rate. If Merck ultimately licenses the MDASE technology — whether voluntarily or by court order — even a mid-single-digit royalty on Keytruda SC global sales could represent hundreds of millions of dollars in annual incremental royalty revenue. At consensus zero.

Merck has stated that it "strongly" disagrees with the German court's ruling, considers Halozyme's patent invalid globally, and is confident it will ultimately prevail. The outcome is genuinely uncertain. But the asymmetry is notable: the current stock price assigns essentially no probability to a favorable resolution, yet even partial success — a settlement, a licensing agreement, or a damage award — could be transformative.

## What Could Go Wrong

**ENHANZE patent cliffs.** The U.S. ENHANZE patent expires in 2027 and the European patent in March 2029. Upon expiry, royalty rates step down in affected markets for products without co-formulation patent protection. Newer products with co-formulation IP (VYVGART, RYBREVANT) extend the full-rate period significantly, but the 2030–2035 royalty trajectory is legitimately uncertain.

**Leverage.** Following the two acquisitions and a November 2025 convertible note issuance, leverage has increased materially. The balance sheet carries approximately $2.2 billion in total convertible debt, with principal maturities across multiple series including 2027, 2028, 2031, and 2032 notes. Cash generation should service this comfortably under the base case, but it constrains capital allocation flexibility and raises the stakes if royalty momentum slows.

**Partner concentration.** Janssen (DARZALEX SC) and argenx (VYVGART Hytrulo) together account for roughly 74% of total royalty revenue. Any clinical setback, competitive pressure, or pricing headwind at either partner would reverberate quickly into Halozyme's royalty line. This concentration is the single most important fundamental risk to monitor.

**MDASE litigation loss.** A ruling against Halozyme's patents — particularly a finding of invalidity — would remove an upside catalyst and potentially signal weakness in the broader MDASE portfolio. The Patent Trial and Appeal Board has instituted post-grant review of certain MDASE patents at Merck's request, adding procedural uncertainty.

**CFO transition.** CFO Nicole LaBrosse is departing, with David Ramsay named as interim CFO effective March 23, 2026. Leadership transitions at the CFO level introduce modest execution risk during a period of elevated complexity from two recent acquisitions.

## Valuation: What's It Worth?

Using the midpoint of 2026 guidance and a current EV of roughly $7.4 billion:

- **2026E EV/Adjusted EBITDA:** ~6.3x (midpoint $1,165M)
- **2026E Forward P/E (Non-GAAP):** ~8.0x (midpoint EPS $8.00)
- **2028E EV/Adjusted EBITDA:** ~4.8x (midpoint $1,528M)

I believe a reasonable range of fair value can be constructed as follows:

**Bear case (12x 2026 EBITDA):** ~$88/share — assumes no credit for Hypercon, conservative ENHANZE cliff assumptions, no MDASE recovery.

**Base case (14x 2026 EBITDA, 20% discount for leverage/cliff risk):** ~$100/share — assigns modest value to Hypercon optionality, partial MDASE recovery, and continued DARZALEX/VYVGART royalty growth.

**Bull case (16x 2026 EBITDA + MDASE licensing value):** $115–130/share — full credit for ENHANZE durability through co-formulation IP, successful Hypercon commercialization, and a MDASE licensing resolution with Merck.

Even the bear case implies approximately 38% upside from current levels. The question is not whether HALO is cheap on a forward earnings basis — it clearly is. The question is whether the market's skepticism about the post-2029 cliff, leverage, and patent durability is well-calibrated or overdone. I believe the evidence increasingly points to the latter.

## Bottom Line

Halozyme sits at one of the more compelling entry points in its recent history. The stock has pulled back 23% from its 52-week high despite delivering record revenues, raising multi-year guidance, adding two strategic platform assets, and scoring a meaningful early win in its patent enforcement campaign against Merck's Keytruda SC.

At roughly 6x forward adjusted EBITDA and 8x forward non-GAAP earnings, the valuation represents a significant discount to what a royalty-dominant, asset-light biopharmaceutical business with visible double-digit earnings growth should command. The thesis is straightforward: own a compounding royalty engine at an undemanding multiple, with a free option on a potentially transformative IP enforcement outcome. The risks — patent cliffs, leverage, partner concentration, and litigation uncertainty — are real but well-understood and, in my view, more than reflected in the current price.
